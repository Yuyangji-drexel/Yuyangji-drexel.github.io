Abstract

Overview: Vision, graphics, and generative models can now reconstruct and synthesize humans with high visual fidelity. However, they rarely model how bodies should move under real-world physical constraints, such as contact, friction, joint limits, muscle effort, ground reaction forces (GRF), and center-of-mass (CoM) dynamics.

This workshop brings together computer vision, biomechanics, simulation, sports/rehabilitation, and XR researchers to make these physics quantities first-class targets for learning from video, IMU, and multimodal data. We will discuss datasets, metrics, and toolchains (e.g., OpenSim, MuJoCo, MyoSuite) that enable benchmarking of physical plausibility, and we will highlight applications in sports, clinical assessment, ergonomics, and safe human‚Äìdigital interaction.

Focus of the Workshop

Physics-Based Estimation

Vision-based estimation of physical quantities: GRF/CoM, joint torques/moments, and contact/friction states from video and sensors.

Human Modeling

Physically grounded 3D human/body/face modeling, including musculoskeletal and soft-tissue (skin-slide) models.

Wearables & Exoskeletons

Modeling human + wearables/exoskeletons/footwear as a coupled physical system for rehabilitation and performance.

Human-Object Interaction

Physics-based contact reasoning, force estimation, and manipulation-aware motion understanding.

Garment & Material

Physics-aware garment modeling for dynamic cloth‚Äìbody interaction, deformation, and draping under physical constraints.

Motion Generation

Physics-aware motion, pose, and avatar generation with joint limits and energetic/effort priors.

Simulation Alignment

Aligning OpenSim, MuJoCo, MyoSuite with in-the-wild video, IMUs, and RGB-D data.

Datasets & Benchmarks

Metrics for physical plausibility: contact-violation, stability indices, GRF error, energy-consistency.
